The African penguin is the only penguin species native to Africa, earning the nickname "Charlie Chaplins of the bird world" due to their endearingly clumsy demeanor. However, these charismatic birds are facing a desperate battle against extinction. Despite the protection of their breeding islands, numbers continue to plummet. Scientists are racing to uncover the causes behind this alarming decline. Could there still be a glimmer of hope for the African Chaplin?

African Penguins Commute Home in Rush Hour

African penguins live amongst the people of busy Cape Town, South Africa. To return to their nests in residential gardens, these penguins huddle together as they cross streets during rush hour traffic.
